AMX-56 Leclerc

The Leclerc is a third generation French main battle tank developed and manufactured by Nexter Systems.It was named in honor of Marshal Philippe Leclerc de Hauteclocque, a commander of the Free French Forces, who led the 2nd Armoured Division in World War II.

Leopard 2

German main tank. The machine development program began in 1970; the first prototype was built in 1972, in 1976 the prototype Leopard 2 AV passed comparative tests with the Abrams prototype at the Aberdeen Proving Ground in the USA; The first serial Leopard 2 tank was handed over to the German army on October 25, 1979.

T-26

T-26 is a Soviet light tank. The most numerous tank of the Red and Finnish Armies by the beginning of World War II and the Army of the Spanish Republic of the Civil War in Spain, the second largest Soviet tank of the 1930s-1940s after the T-34.

T-34

T-34 - Soviet medium tank of the Great Patriotic War period, mass-produced since 1940. During 1942-1947 - the main tank of the Red Army and the USSR Armed Forces. It was the main tank of the Red Army until the first half of 1944, before its modification T-34-85 entered the troops.

The most massive tank of the Second World War and the post-war period.

Cruiser Mk.I, A9

The Tank was a British cruiser tank of the interwar period. It was the first cruiser tank: a fast tank designed to bypass the main enemy lines and engage the enemy's lines of communication, as well as enemy tanks. The Cruiser Mk II was a more heavily armoured adaptation of the Mark I, developed at much the same time.

M3 stuart

The M3 Stuart/Light Tank M3, was an American light tank of World War II. An improved version of the tank entered service as the M5 in 1942 to be supplied to British and other Commonwealth forces under lend-lease prior to the entry of the U.S. into the war. Afterwards, it was used by U.S. and Allied forces until the end of the war.

M2

US medium tank during World War II. It is the direct predecessor of the M3 General Lee tank. Morally obsolete even before the start of serial production due to the imperfection of the hull design and weak armament. It never participated in hostilities, it was used as a training tank in the United States.

M4 Sherman

The M4 Sherman, officially Medium Tank, M4, was the most widely used medium tank by the United States and Western Allies in World War II. The M4 Sherman proved to be reliable, relatively cheap to produce, and available in great numbers. It was also the basis of several other armored fighting vehicles including self-propelled artillery, tank destroyers, and armored recovery vehicles.

Tens of thousands were distributed through the Lend-Lease program to the British Commonwealth and Soviet Union. The tank was named by the British after the American Civil War General William Tecumseh Sherman.

Char B1

French medium tank of the 1930s. Developed since 1921, it was adopted in March 1934. During serial production from 1935 to June 15, 1940, 403 B1 tanks were produced in various variants. B1 was actively used in battles with German troops in May - June 1940, despite the rather archaic design, the tank showed excellent protection.

After the surrender of France, almost half of the machines produced (161 units) were captured by the Wehrmacht and marked according to the departmental rubric of the Ministry of Armaments. Pz. Kpfw. B2 740 (f) was used by him until 1945, also serving as the basis for the creation of 16 self-propelled artillery installations and 60 flame-throwing tanks at their base.

Pz.Kpfw. III

Panzerkampfwagen III is a German medium tank of the Second World War, serially produced from 1937 to 1943. The abbreviated names of this tank were Pz.Kpfw.III, Panzer III, Pz.III. In the departmental rubric of military equipment of Nazi Germany, this tank had the designation Sd.Kfz. 141 (Sonderkraftfahrzeug 141 — special purpose vehicle 141).

These combat vehicles were used by the Wehrmacht from the first day of World War II. The last records of the combat use of Pz.Kpfw.III in the regular composition of Wehrmacht divisions date from the middle of 1944, single tanks fought until the capitulation of Germany.

Panzer IV

It was the main and most massive tank of the Wehrmacht. For the German army, it had the same meaning as the T-34 for the USSR and the M4 Sherman for the USA. The constant improvement of the armor and armament of the tank allowed it to be a worthy competitor to them throughout the war.

Tiger 1

Panzerkampfwagen VI Ausf.H - E, "Tiger" - a German heavy tank of the Second World War, the prototype of which was the VK4501 (H) tank, developed in 1942 by Henschel. Along with the prototype of the Henschel company, the Reich leadership was also presented with the project of Ferdinand Porsche - VK4501 (P), but the choice of the military commission fell on the Henschel option.

For the first time, Tiger tanks took part in the hostilities on August 29, 1942 near the Mga station near Leningrad, and began to be massively used during the capture of Kharkov in February-March 1943. Used by the Wehrmacht and SS troops until the end of World War II.
